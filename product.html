TYPICAL FOOD PER REGION



NORD-ITALY

Lasagna

"Fresh pasta accompanied by one of the most well-known condiments in the world: Bolognese ragù. Bologna, the cradle of one of the richest and most enticing regional cuisines in our country, is the capital of ragù. The recipe for baked lasagna with Bolognese ragù involves a sauce made from a sauté of celery, onion, and carrot, to which finely minced beef and often small additions of pork and tomato paste are then added. The composition of the lasagna is therefore a combination of layers of porous and thin egg pasta alternated with meat ragù, béchamel, and Parmesan cheese."

Risotto

In the cuisine of Northern Italy, it's ubiquitous. It's also found beyond borders: Ticinese-style risotto, with red wine, cheese, luganighetta sausage, or porcini mushrooms. An excellence is that with the pumpkin from Mantua or the Venetian ones with red Treviso radicchio or with peas "Risi e Bisi". Books from Milan, the City-State, delivered to your home: discover how to do it Perhaps the most representative risotto of Italian cuisine, certainly of Lombard and Milanese cuisine, is nonetheless risotto alla milanese, recognized worldwide as the quintessential saffron risotto. Its distinguishing feature is mainly the presence of marrow. The ingredients to make this exquisite dish are rice, grated cheese, butter, veal marrow, beef broth, onion, and saffron, preferably extracted directly from the pistils steeped in infusion.

Tiramisù

The most beloved Italian dessert in the world has seen its paternity contested for decades, mainly between Veneto and Friuli Venezia Giulia. In fact, while the traditional Friulian recipe has been included by the Ministry of Agricultural, Food, and Forestry Policies in the list of traditional Friulian agri-food products, in Treviso, where the Venetians claim the dessert was conceived, the Tiramisù World Cup has been held for some years now. The main ingredients are ladyfinger biscuits, mascarpone cheese, coffee, and cocoa.

MID-ITALY

Carbonara

The porchetta from Ariccia, a Protected Geographical Indication (PGI) product, as well as the white pizza, make Lazio and its capital city a significant culinary destination. Particularly unique and worth trying is the "tiella," prepared in a pan, typically cooked in Gaeta. A rustic pizza, unmistakable, often filled with fish, octopus, or escarole, but also delicious with anchovies, cod, mussels, and small squid. A must-try in this region of Italy is a good plate of spaghetti carbonara, gricia, or amatriciana.

Bistecca alla fiorentina

A quintessential dish that embodies the essence of Tuscan cuisine. This culinary masterpiece features a thick cut of veal or heifer steak, meticulously selected for its quality and tenderness. Prepared with utmost care, the steak is grilled to perfection, reaching a delicate balance between succulence and charred perfection. Cooking temperatures range between 48°C for those who prefer their meat rare, allowing the rich juices to flow freely, to 65°C for those who savor a well-done steak, ensuring a satisfying tenderness and depth of flavor. This iconic dish is a testament to the artistry of Tuscan cuisine, captivating the hearts and palates of meat enthusiasts around the world. Bistecca alla Fiorentina is not merely a meal; it is an exquisite journey for the senses, a celebration of tradition, and a culinary experience like no other.

Tagliatelle al tartufo

A culinary delight that epitomizes the sophistication of Italian cuisine. This iconic dish features delicate ribbons of freshly-made tagliatelle pasta, generously adorned with the prized delicacy known as tartufo, or truffle. Crafted with precision and care, each strand of pasta is meticulously coated in a velvety sauce infused with the earthy aroma and rich flavor of black or white truffles, sourced from the pristine forests of Italy. Savoring Tagliatelle al Tartufo is a sensory journey like no other. With every bite, the palate is greeted by the luxurious essence of truffles, dancing harmoniously with the tender texture of the pasta. Each mouthful is a symphony of flavors, a celebration of Italy's culinary heritage and the art of indulgence. Whether enjoyed as a decadent entrée or the star attraction of a gourmet feast, Tagliatelle al Tartufo captivates the senses and leaves an indelible impression on all who have the pleasure of experiencing its exquisite allure.

SUD-ITALY

Pizza

An iconic dish that transcends borders and tantalizes taste buds worldwide. Originating from the vibrant streets of Naples, Italy, pizza has evolved into a culinary sensation cherished by millions. At its heart lies a simple yet harmonious combination of ingredients: a thin, crispy crust adorned with a vibrant tomato sauce, creamy mozzarella cheese, and a medley of toppings limited only by imagination. From classic Margherita to adventurous creations featuring gourmet ingredients, pizza offers a symphony of flavors to suit every palate. Baked to perfection in a fiery oven, pizza emerges with a tantalizing aroma and golden crust that beckons eager diners. Each slice promises a delightful interplay of textures and tastes, from the savory richness of the cheese to the tangy sweetness of the tomato sauce. More than just a meal, pizza is a cultural icon that brings people together, sparking lively conversations and fostering a sense of camaraderie. Whether enjoyed in a bustling pizzeria in Naples or savored in the comfort of one's home, pizza transcends mere food—it is an experience, a celebration of culinary artistry, and a testament to the universal love for good food and good company.

Pescato

Fish is a culinary treasure trove enriched by the bountiful waters of the Mediterranean Sea. From the sun-kissed shores of Sicily to the picturesque coastlines of Puglia and Calabria, Southern Italy boasts a diverse array of seafood delicacies that captivate the senses and delight the palate. Freshness is paramount in Southern Italian seafood cuisine, with fishermen bringing in their daily catch of branzino, pesce spada (swordfish), gamberi (shrimp), calamari (squid), and a variety of flavorful shellfish. Each specimen is carefully selected for its quality and flavor potential, ensuring a dining experience that is second to none. The preparation of Southern Italian fish dishes is a testament to centuries-old culinary traditions, where simplicity and respect for ingredients reign supreme. Whether grilled to perfection and drizzled with fragrant olive oil, gently poached in a savory broth, or lovingly incorporated into pasta dishes bursting with Mediterranean flavors, Southern Italian seafood celebrates the natural essence of the sea. Savoring fish from Southern Italy is more than just a meal—it is an immersive journey through the coastal landscapes and cultural tapestries of the region. With every bite, one can taste the salty breeze of the sea, feel the warmth of the Mediterranean sun, and experience the vibrant spirit of Southern Italian cuisine in all its glory.

Arancino

A beloved Sicilian delicacy that embodies the essence of Italian comfort food. These golden orbs of delight are as visually striking as they are flavorful, capturing the hearts and palates of food enthusiasts worldwide. Originating from the sun-drenched island of Sicily, arancini are a culinary marvel crafted from leftover risotto rice, which is lovingly shaped into balls, stuffed with a tantalizing filling, coated in breadcrumbs, and deep-fried to crispy perfection. The name "arancini" is derived from the Italian word "arancia," meaning orange, owing to their resemblance to the fruit when fried to a golden hue. However, despite their name, these savory treats are far from fruity; they are filled with a savory medley of ingredients such as ragù (meat sauce), mozzarella cheese, peas, and saffron-infused rice, creating a symphony of flavors in every bite. Sinking your teeth into a freshly fried arancino is an experience like no other. The crunchy exterior gives way to a creamy, aromatic center, bursting with the rich flavors of Sicilian cuisine. Each bite is a journey through the vibrant streets of Palermo or Catania, where arancini are enjoyed as a cherished snack or part of a hearty meal. Whether savored as a street food indulgence or served as an elegant appetizer at a gourmet dinner party, arancini are a true testament to the artistry and ingenuity of Sicilian gastronomy. With their irresistible allure and timeless appeal, these delectable rice balls continue to enchant food lovers of all ages, making them a beloved symbol of Sicilian culinary heritage.
